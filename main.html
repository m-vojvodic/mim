Who Are We

The Electronic Dance Music Club (EDMC) at UCLA is an on-campus organization, founded in 2012 that aims to provide DJ's, producers, and fans with a safe space to learn and practice their skills and discuss their interests in the growing music field around us. We share our interests and talents with the rest of campus and the Los Angeles community by hosting and DJ'ing various events on campus and around LA. We have also been able to bring popular artists to our campus for student enjoyment, some of which including Singularity, Nutrik, and Paul Oakenfold. As a club, we have grown exponentially in the last couple years. Starting last year, we felt that we could use our talents for something greater than ourselves. And thus, we put together a campaign to benefit the community.

For more information please visit our website.

Started in 2014

Message in a Melody is a fundraising campaign and benefit concert created by the Electronic Dance Music Club at UCLA. This idea was spearheaded by Elizabeth Matusov who was inspired by the courage her high school friend, Ashley Jensen-Pray, showed in her fight against a rare form of soft tissue cancer. Although Ashley passed away in December 2013, her story lives on with Message in a Melody, touching the hearts of all those around her and the entire UCLA community.

All members in EDMC have been personally affected by cancer, whether through friends or family members. Inspired by Ashley, EDMC members took action as an UCLA student group to reach out and bring forth this powerful cause to our fellow peers and inspire change.

Last year, Message in a Melody's benefit concert was hosted in the Ackerman Grand Ballroom, located on the UCLA Campus on May 26, 2014. Featuring DJ and producer Kastle and two student DJ's, BVSD (Marko Vojvodic) and Thom Rugo (Philip Scott), the benefit concert was an incredible success, with over 250 attendees. A cancer wall was also created in the time leading up to the benefit concert, allowing the UCLA community to sign the huge wall for anyone they know that has been affected by cancer. This aggregation of support was displayed on the night of the benefit concert, showing the strength in our community against cancer. We are extremely proud to say that the entire fundraising campaign, which includes the benefit concert, has raised over $5,000 for the Department of Pediatric Oncology at Mattel Children's Hospital.

This Year: 2015

Inspired by the success of last year's campaign, Message in a Melody 2015 will continue to raise funds and awareness for pediatric aids. Whereas last year's team only consisted of five amazing members, we've expanded our entire staff to twenty individuals. We are also shifting the campaign's focus from music to education and awareness on childhood cancer, although music is still an integral part of Message in a Melody. Due to the increase of staff members, we are able to create a full-blown campaign with many more organized events leading up to the benefit concert, such as a Kerckhoff Art Gallery Showing, _________________, and transitioning to UCLA Spark as our crowdsourcing platform to further engage UCLA students and alumni. This year's benefit concert will be in Ackerman Grand Ballroom, located on the UCLA campus, on May 6, 2015. Our goal is to surpass the amount of donations fundraised last year and evoke an even greater engagement in the UCLA community against pediatric cancer.

Our Mission

To support the eradication of childhood cancer by raising money for research at the Department of Pediatric Oncology at Mattel Children's Hospital and to inspire the UCLA community to take action against pediatric cancer.

Where Funds Will Go

Fundraising efforts and donations will support the research of Dr. Theodore Moore, M.D., chief of pediatric hematology-oncology at Mattel Children's Hospital, whose cell therapy clinics strives to help bring the recovery rate of pediatric cancer up to 100 percent. Dr. Moore worked with Ashley Jensen-Pray at Mattel Children's Hospital and his research is severely underfunded.
